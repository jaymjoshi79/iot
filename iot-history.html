The Evolution of IoT

From Connected Computers to a Connected World

Timeline of IoT Evolution

1982

The First Internet-Connected Appliance

Carnegie Mellon University students modified a Coca-Cola vending machine to report its inventory and whether newly loaded drinks were cold, marking one of the first IoT devices.

1999

Term "Internet of Things" Coined

Kevin Ashton, working at Procter & Gamble, coined the term "Internet of Things" while working on supply chain optimization using RFID technology.

2008-2009

IoT Reaches "More Things Than People"

The number of connected devices exceeded the world's population for the first time, marking a significant milestone in IoT adoption.

2011-2015

Rise of Smart Home Technology

Introduction of popular smart home devices and virtual assistants revolutionized home automation and brought IoT into everyday life.

Impact Across Industries

Healthcare

Remote patient monitoring, smart medical devices, and automated health tracking have transformed patient care and medical research.

Manufacturing

Industrial IoT (IIoT) has enabled predictive maintenance, automated quality control, and optimized production processes.

Agriculture

Smart farming technologies have improved crop yields, reduced water usage, and enabled precision agriculture.

The Future of IoT

As we move forward, IoT continues to evolve with emerging technologies like 5G, AI, and edge computing. The future promises even greater connectivity, efficiency, and innovation across all aspects of our lives.
