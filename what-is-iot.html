What is IoT?

Understanding the Internet of Things and its Impact on Our World

Understanding IoT

The Internet of Things (IoT) refers to the vast network of physical objects embedded with sensors, software, and other technologies that enable them to connect and exchange data with other devices and systems over the internet.

Key Definition

"IoT describes the network of physical objects that are embedded with sensors, software, and other technologies for the purpose of connecting and exchanging data with other devices and systems over the internet."

Core Components of IoT

Sensors/Devices

Collection of data from the environment or object under measurement using various types of sensors

Connectivity

Sending collected data to cloud infrastructure through various methods of transmission

Data Processing

Processing and analyzing data to make valuable insights

User Interface

Making information available to end-users in an easy to understand format

Key Characteristics

Interconnectivity

Everything in IoT is able to communicate with global information and service infrastructure

Things-related Services

Capable of providing thing-related services within constraints like privacy protection

Heterogeneity

Devices in IoT are heterogeneous based on different hardware platforms and networks

Dynamic Changes

Device state changes dynamically, including location, speed, and connected networks
